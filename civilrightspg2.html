Since the death of Trayvon Martin, the world has started clearly seeing an issue that has been going on in America since the founding of this country. Over the last 8 years, we have seen a call for change that has lit a fire in the minds and hearts of millions of people.
A note: The following timeline does not begin to touch the number of victims robbed of their lives by police in the US.
  • 2012: The murder of Trayvon Martin.
  • 2013: Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors, and Opal Tometi created Black Lives Matter.
  • 2014: The murder of Eric Garner, Michael Brown, John Crawford III, and Tamir Rice.
  • 2015: The murder of Anthony Hill, Walter Scott, Freddie Gray, and Sandra Bland.
  • 2016: The murder of Deborah Danner, Alton Sterling, Philando Castile, and Korryn Gaines. Colin Kaepernick takes a knee during the National Anthem on September 1st during a pre-season NFL game.
  • 2017: Minnesota officer who killed Philando Castile found Not Guilty. Charlottesville white nationalist rally.
  • 2018: The murder of Grechario Mark and Kenneth Ross Jr.
  • 2019: The murder of Isaiah Lewis and Elijah McClain.
  • 2020: The murder of Ahumaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, Daniel Prude, George Floyd, and Tony McDade. Justice For George Floyd gains worldwide momentum. Jacob Blake is shot by police and paralyzed.
It would be nearly impossible to include every individual wrongfully murdered by police since 2000. However, the next page goes over some of the steps towards justice that have been taken since the death of Trayvon Martin.
